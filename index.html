Alan Rickman

"A magnificent actor & a wonderful man." -J.K. Rowling

Alan Rickman, left, first developed his passion for the arts at Latymer Upper School, where he was a student for 8 years.

Here is a timeline of Alan Rickman's life:

  • 1946 - Born in Acton, London
  • 1956 - Wins a scholarship to Latymer Upper School, with his first dramatic role in a school production of Shakespeare's Coriolanus, playing the title character's mother
  • 1964 - Studies graphic design at Chelsea College of Art and Design, where he meets his future wife Rima Horton
  • 1967 - Enrolls in graduate studies at the Royal College of Art, working as a graphic designer for Notting Hill Herald. "Drama school wasn't considered the sensible thing to do at 18," he recalled during an interview in 2011.
  • 1969 - Opens a successful graphic design studio, Graphiti, with several friends and runs it for 3 years
  • 1972 - Decides to pursue acting professionally, requesting an audition with the Royal Academy of Dramatic Art Graduates after 2 years of studies and supporting himself working as a dresser for senior actors
  • 1974-1985 - Performs with several British repertory and experimental theatre groups such as the Court Drama Group and the acclaimed Royal Shakespeare Company, the latter of which he disliked the experience. "It's a factory," he said, "It has to be. It's all about product endlessly churned out--not sufficiently about process. They don't look after the young actors....There ought to be someone who helps them develop."
  • 1985 - Plays the male lead, Le Vicompte de Valmont, in the RSC production of Les Liaisons Dangereuses, for which he receives a Tony Award nomination and Drama Desk Award nomination when the production moves to Broadway
  • 1988 - Makes his film debut as legendary villain Hans Gruber in Die Hard, earning the 46th spot on the American Film Institute's list of the "100 Best Heroes/Villains"
  • 1993 - Gets elected to the Council of the Royal Academy of Dramatic Art, later becoming RADA's Vice-Chairman, its Artistic Advisory and Training Development Board; his desire to help struggling performers leads him to be Honorary President of IPAT in later years.
  • 1999 - Plays Sir Alexander Dane/Dr. Lazarus in the sci-fi adventure/parody film Galaxy Quest, a role seriously deserving of an Oscar according to this writer
  • 2001-2011 - Portrays the iconic role of Severus Snape in the Harry Potter film franchise, earning several award nominations for Best Supporting Actor
  • 2012 - Marries longtime partner Rima Horton in a private ceremony in New York City
  • 2015 - Tapes a voiceover for This Tortoise Could Save a Life, a short film in aid of the Save the Children and Refugee Council--this was his final job.
  • 2016 - Dies at the age of 69 from a secret battle with pancreatic cancer

"Actors are agents of change. A film, a piece of theatre, a piece of music, or a book can make a difference. It can change the world."

Alan Rickman
